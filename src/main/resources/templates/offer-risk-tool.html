product thesis

This should feel like a pre-sign RN decision desk, not a calculator.

OfferVerdict is for one moment: a nurse has a real offer in hand and needs to know whether the written terms change the decision to sign, negotiate, relocate, or walk away without regretting the unit later.

1Start from the nurse's raw input, even when it is only a messy note.
2Separate guaranteed money from shift-dependent upside.
3Price the downside hiding in clawbacks, float, cancellations, relocation, and unit survivability.
4Return sign, negotiate, or walk away plus the next message to send right now.

specialty entry points

Different RN units fail in different ways.

The same hourly rate means something different in ICU, ED, med-surg/tele, or L&D. The packet now pushes that context higher instead of treating every RN offer the same.

icu High-acuity support versus ICU pay

Orientation depth, preceptor support, float expectations, and charge coverage can matter more than another few dollars an hour.

 ed Boarding, violence, and ED chaos load

Emergency offers can look attractive until boarding burden, psych volume, security, and throughput pressure show up.

 med-surg / tele Total-care load and turnover pressure

Admits, discharges, telemetry burden, and CNA support can decide whether the rate is actually survivable.

 l&d Induction, triage, OR coverage, and fetal monitoring

L&D offers break differently. The real risk is often support structure and acuity coverage, not just the posted number.

 new grad First offer support versus lock-in

Residency, preceptor quality, and repayment terms matter more when the nurse is entering the unit for the first time.

 travel Guaranteed hours and cancellation risk

Travel contracts need protection around stipends, cancellation, float scope, and assignment changes.
