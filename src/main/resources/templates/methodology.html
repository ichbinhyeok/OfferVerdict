Tool About
Methodology

How the offer risk report is calculated.

OfferVerdict separates guaranteed pay from conditional upside, then estimates whether the move leaves enough monthly cash to justify relocation and contract risk.

Pay stack

Annual pay is decomposed before judgment

Base pay is hourly rate multiplied by scheduled weekly hours. Overtime is modeled at 1.5x hourly rate. Night and weekend differentials are modeled only for the hours entered, so variable upside does not hide the base-rate reality.

offer pay = base + overtime + night diff + weekend diff
Cash reality

Monthly residual uses the existing tax and city engine

The model estimates take-home pay, local rent, insurance, and baseline living costs for both the current city and offer city. The main decision metric is the monthly residual delta after those costs.

delta = offer residual - current residual
Contract risk

Bonus and relocation money are treated as refundable risk

Sign-on bonus and relocation stipend are reduced by an estimated supplemental tax haircut, then compared against moving costs. If the planned stay is shorter than the commitment period, the tool estimates repayment exposure using full or prorated repayment logic.

risk = repayment exposure + upfront cash gap
Verdict

The output is intentionally blunt

The report says acceptable, negotiate, or walk away. A strong monthly gain can still be a negotiate verdict if repayment exposure, relocation gaps, or non-guaranteed differential assumptions are too large.

break-even = risk / monthly delta

What this does not know

It does not read your actual contract, unit policy, tax situation, family expenses, neighborhood choice, license costs, or personal debt. Treat the report as a structured first pass, then verify the legal and tax details before signing.
